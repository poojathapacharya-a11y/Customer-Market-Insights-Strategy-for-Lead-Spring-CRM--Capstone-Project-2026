# ABS Critical Analysis for Lead Spring CRM

Source files used:

- `8165DC01.xlsx` - ABS Counts of Australian Businesses, including Entries and Exits, June 2021 to June 2025
- `8165DC04.xlsx` - Survival of Businesses by Main State by Subdivision by Employment Size Ranges
- `8165DC06.xlsx` - Survival of Businesses by Main State by Subdivision by Turnover Size Ranges

## Key Findings

Western Australia is a credible market for Lead Spring CRM. ABS data shows WA reached 266,273 operating businesses by June 2025, with 43,723 business entries in 2024-25 and annual growth of 4.3%.

The opportunity is not only growth, but also churn. WA recorded 33,190 exits in 2024-25, meaning many businesses are entering the market while many others are failing or closing. This supports the business case for CRM, automation, follow-up systems, and marketing discipline.

Business survival data confirms the problem. Only 64.1% of WA businesses operating in June 2021 survived to June 2025. For new WA entries from 2021-22, only 51.8% survived to June 2025. This suggests early-stage and small businesses need better customer management and retention systems.

The employment-size data supports an SME-focused strategy. Target segments such as Professional + IT Services, Finance, Medical/Health Care, Property/Real Estate, Food/Beverage, and Construction are mostly small businesses under 20 employees.

Turnover data suggests price sensitivity. Most target segments have a high proportion of businesses below $2m annual turnover, so Lead Spring CRM should not rely only on high-cost Premium packages. Starter and Growth packages are more realistic for market entry, with Premium reserved for higher-value professional, finance, IT, and property clients.

## Strategic Recommendation

The strongest initial target segments are:

1. Professional + IT Services
2. Finance + Insurance Services
3. Medical + Health Care
4. Property / Real Estate
5. Construction Services

Professional/IT and Finance have the strongest strategic fit because they combine strong business numbers, CRM relevance, and higher digital readiness. Construction has the largest business count, but may require simpler messaging and lower-cost entry packages due to lower digital readiness.
